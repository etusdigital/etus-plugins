## Introduction

"We're now moving to vision synthesis. I'll ask you structured questions about your ideal future state, business objectives, target users, and differentiation. Then we'll run a brainstorming technique to creatively explore opportunities from your problems. This becomes your guiding document for all downstream planning."

**Prerequisites:** project-context.md exists and is approved.

**Duration:** ~40 minutes total
**Output:** product-vision.md with BO-1, BO-2, BO-3... identifiers

---

## Pre-Interview Setup

**Load Context:**
"Let me reference your project context... [reads key points]. Okay, building on that foundation, let's define your vision."

## Block 1: Vision (2 questions)

### Q1: Ideal Future State
**Question:** "Imagine it's 2 years from now and your product is wildly successful. What does that future look like? Paint me a picture."

**Listen for:** Inspired language, strategic outcomes, user transformation, market position.

**Record:**
- Future state narrative: [user input]

---

### Q2: One-Line Vision
**Question:** "Now distill that into one sentence. What is the vision in a single line?"

**Listen for:** Clarity, inspiration, specificity (not generic).

**Examples:**
- "Enable solo freelancers to get paid faster by automating invoice follow-ups."
- "Turn every small business owner into a data analyst."
- "Make cybersecurity invisible to end users."

**Record:**
- Vision statement: [user input]

---

### Q1 Confirmation
> **Confirm Understanding:** "So your vision is [VISION_STATEMENT]. The future state looks like [FUTURE_NARRATIVE]. Does that feel right?"

## Block 2: Business Objectives (3 questions)

### Q3: Success Definition
**Question:** "What does success look like for this business? What are the 2-3 most important things you want to achieve?"

**Listen for:** Strategic outcomes (growth, profitability, user satisfaction, market share, impact).

**Record:**
- Success factor 1: [user input]
- Success factor 2: [user input]
- Success factor 3: [user input]

---

### Q4: Measurable Goals
**Question:** "Now let's get specific. For each of those success factors, what's the metric and the target? Example: 'We want to hit $100K MRR by month 12' or '10K active users in year 1.'"

**Listen for:** Quantified targets, realistic timelines, business metrics.

**Anti-pattern:** Vague targets like "grow a lot" → Push for numbers.

**Record:**
- BO-1 name: [Factor 1]
  - Metric 1: [target] by [date]
  - Metric 2: [target] by [date]
- BO-2 name: [Factor 2]
  - Metric 1: [target] by [date]
  - Metric 2: [target] by [date]
- BO-3 name: [Factor 3]
  - Metric 1: [target] by [date]
  - Metric 2: [target] by [date]

---

### Q5: Strategic Timeline & Prioritization
**Question:** "If you could only hit ONE of these 3 objectives, which would it be? That's your North Star."

**Listen for:** True priority, trade-offs they'd accept.

**Record:**
- North Star metric: [Which objective is #1?]

---

### Q2 Confirmation
> **Confirm Understanding:** "So your business objectives are:
> - BO-1: [OBJECTIVE 1] → [METRICS] by [TIMELINE]
> - BO-2: [OBJECTIVE 2] → [METRICS] by [TIMELINE]
> - BO-3: [OBJECTIVE 3] → [METRICS] by [TIMELINE]
>
> Your North Star is [NORTH_STAR]. Is that right?"

- **Secondary Persona:**
  - Name: [user input]
  - Profile: [user input]

### Q7: Value Proposition
**Question:** "For your primary persona, what specific value do you provide? Not 'better tools' but 'save 5 hours/week' or 'increase revenue by 20%' or 'reduce risk by 80%.'"

**Listen for:** Quantified, specific, outcome-focused value.

**Record:**
- Value provided: [user input]
- For whom specifically: [user input]
- Why they should choose you over alternatives: [user input]

### Q3 Confirmation
> **Confirm Understanding:** "Your primary persona is [PERSONA]. They struggle with [PAIN]. Your value to them is [VALUE]. Right?"

## Block 4: Differentiation (2 questions)

### Q8: Current Alternatives
**Question:** "What do your users do today instead of using your product? What are the top 2-3 alternatives they're comparing you against (competitors, DIY solutions, manual workarounds)?"

**Listen for:** Direct competitors, indirect competitors (alternative approaches), status quo.

**Record:**
- Alternative 1: [name] → Strengths: [user input] → Weaknesses: [user input]
- Alternative 2: [name] → Strengths: [user input] → Weaknesses: [user input]
- Alternative 3: [name] → Strengths: [user input] → Weaknesses: [user input]

---

### Q9: Differentiation
**Question:** "Why would someone choose YOU over those alternatives? What's your unfair advantage?"

**Listen for:** Product differentiation, go-to-market differentiation, team differentiation, business model differentiation.

**Record:**
- Unique advantage 1: [user input]
- Unique advantage 2: [user input]
- How defensible is this?: [user input]

---

### Q4 Confirmation
> **Confirm Understanding:** "So you compete against [ALT1], [ALT2], [ALT3]. Your competitive advantage is [ADV1] and [ADV2]. Is that accurate?"

## Block 5: HMW Transformation & Brainstorming (10 mins)

### Explain HMW Technique
"Now I want to introduce a technique called 'How Might We.' It transforms problems into opportunity-focused questions. It's great for creative exploration."

**Example:**
- Problem: "Freelancers spend 5 hours/week chasing unpaid invoices."
- HMW: "How might we eliminate the need for freelancers to follow up on invoices?"

**Why it works:** Shifts from complaint to opportunity. Unlocks creative solutions.

---

### Q10: Co-Create HMW Questions
**Question:** "Let's transform your top 3 problems from project-context into HMW questions. What's problem #1?"

**User provides problem.** You reframe together:
- **Draft HMW:** "How might we [reframe of problem]?"
- **User feedback:** "Does that feel right or would you rephrase it?"
- **Refine together**

**Repeat for problems 2 and 3.**

**Record:**
- HMW-1: [co-created question]
- HMW-2: [co-created question]
- HMW-3: [co-created question]

---

### Offer Brainstorm Technique Selection
"Now let's explore ideas using a brainstorming technique. I have 4 techniques we could run:

1. **SCAMPER** (15 min) — Apply 7 prompts (Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to use, Eliminate, Reverse) to your product idea. Great for generating feature ideas.

2. **Reverse Brainstorming** (15 min) — 'How can we make this WORSE?' Then invert for solutions. Great for finding risks and safeguards.

3. **Six Thinking Hats** (20 min) — Role-play 6 perspectives (Facts, Emotions, Pessimism, Optimism, Creativity, Process). Great for well-rounded analysis.

4. **Future State Ideal** (15 min) — 'If there were NO constraints, what would perfect look like?' Then work backward to constraints. Great for aspirational design.

Which sounds most useful for your product right now?"

**Listen for:** User choice.

---

### Execute Chosen Technique

**SCAMPER Example (if chosen):**

"Let's run SCAMPER on your [PRODUCT_NAME] idea. For each prompt, I'll ask 'How might we [prompt] to create better value?'"

1. **Substitute:** "What could we substitute? (e.g., swap manual tracking for automation, replace email with SMS, etc.)"
   - User answers: [input]

2. **Combine:** "What could we combine? (e.g., merge invoice tracking with expense tracking, add time tracking, etc.)"
   - User answers: [input]

3. **Adapt:** "What could we adapt from other industries? (e.g., how does Amazon handle order tracking? How does Stripe handle webhook reliability?)"
   - User answers: [input]

4. **Modify:** "What could we modify? (e.g., change frequency, scale, attributes)"
   - User answers: [input]

5. **Put to use:** "What new uses could we find? (e.g., invoice tracking for accountants, not just freelancers)"
   - User answers: [input]

6. **Eliminate:** "What could we eliminate? (e.g., dashboard, manual inputs, approval workflows)"
   - User answers: [input]

7. **Reverse:** "What could we reverse or rearrange? (e.g., instead of reminding users, remind debtors)"
   - User answers: [input]

**Synthesize insights from all 7.**

---

### Offer Another Technique
"Great! I've captured those insights. Want to run another technique to explore more angles, or shall we move on?"

**If YES:** Offer remaining techniques, repeat execution.
**If NO:** Continue to Block 6.

**Question:** "What's your biggest assumption about this business? What would make you completely rethink your strategy?"

**Listen for:** Riskiest belief, validation method needed.

**Record:**

**Record:**

- MVP must-have 2: [user input]
- MVP must-have 3: [user input]
- Can cut from v1: [user input]

### Q5 Confirmation & Wrap

## Quality Checklist

Before generating the document, verify:

- [ ] Vision statement is clear and inspiring?
- [ ] Business objectives (BO-#) are specific with measurable targets?
- [ ] Target personas are well-defined?
- [ ] Value proposition is quantified?
- [ ] Differentiation is defensible?
- [ ] HMW questions are creative but grounded?
- [ ] Brainstorm technique ran and insights captured?
- [ ] Critical assumption identified with validation method?
- [ ] MVP scope clearly bounded?
- [ ] All blocks confirmed by user?

---

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

❌ **Vague Metrics:** "Lots of users" → Push for numbers: "How many users by when?"

❌ **Skip Brainstorming:** Brainstorming unlocks creative thinking. Don't skip it.

❌ **One Persona:** Different users have different pains. Define primary AND secondary.

❌ **Weak Differentiation:** "Better, faster, cheaper" → Dig deeper: "What specifically? Why can't they do it themselves?"

❌ **Assumptions Without Validation Plans:** Every assumption needs a validation method.
